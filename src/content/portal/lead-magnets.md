# Fast Web Guru — Lead Magnet Strategy
## Corey Haines Lead Magnets Framework

*Created: 2026-06-07 | Source: product-marketing.md + ai-assessment-tool-design.md + lead-magnets skill*

---

## Primary Lead Magnet: The FWG AI Snapshot Assessment

**Type:** Interactive assessment tool (see `ai-assessment-tool-design.md` for full design)
**Buyer stage:** Awareness → Consideration
**Format:** Form-based (name, email, website URL, business type) → personalised email report
**Time to consume:** 2-minute read
**Time to create value:** 30 seconds to submit, 24hr delivery (MVP)

The AI Snapshot is already designed and ready to build. This document covers the distribution, promotion, and gating strategy.

---

## Why This Lead Magnet Works for FWG

Against the lead magnet principles:

✅ **Solves a specific problem** — "I don't know if my website is ready for the AI era" is a precise, timely anxiety
✅ **High perceived value, low time investment** — personalised report with specific findings > generic checklist
✅ **Natural path to product** — the assessment literally surfaces the problems FWG solves; the final section is "what Fast Web Guru would do"
✅ **Easy to consume** — one email, 4 sections, 2-minute read
✅ **Matches the buyer stage** — awareness/consideration; not a "ready to buy" tool

---

## Gating Strategy

**Approach: Full gate (email required before delivery)**

Rationale:
- The output is personalised to their specific website — it genuinely requires their details to produce
- Visitors who submit their URL are highly qualified leads (actively interested in their website)
- The value exchange is transparent: "Enter your details → get a personalised assessment"
- No "preview before gating" needed because the input form IS the preview

**Fields to gate:**
- Name (enables personalisation in email)
- Email (delivery + sequence)
- Website URL (enables real analysis)
- Business type dropdown (enables personalised "biggest opportunity" section)

**Fields to NOT add:**
- Phone number
- Company size
- Budget
- "How did you hear about us"

**Trust copy under the submit button:**
> No sales call. No lock-in. Your assessment lands in your inbox within 24 hours.

## Thank You Page (Post-Submission)

After clicking "Send me my assessment":

**Headline:** You're in. We're on it.

**Body:**
> We've got your details and we're taking a look at [website URL]. Your FWG AI Snapshot will be in your inbox within 24 hours — usually faster.
>
> While you wait, here's what's actually going to be in it: your AI readiness score, three specific quick wins for your website, and the single biggest AI opportunity for your type of business.
>
> If you want to get a head start on what we do: [See how Fast Web Guru works →]

**Secondary CTA:** "See how Fast Web Guru works →" → links to /how-it-works

This keeps the visitor on the site and warm while they wait.

## Distribution Plan

### 1. On-site (primary)
- CTA button on every page: "Get Your Free AI Assessment"
- Inline CTA mid-page on: Homepage (after problem section), Pricing (FAQ bottom), How It Works (after Step 1)
- Exit-intent popup (post-launch, Step 2): "Before you go — get a free look at your website" → `/get-assessment`

### 2. Email to existing 20 hosting clients (launch)
Subject: `[First Name] — we rebuilt Fast Web Guru. Here's your free AI assessment.`
This is the highest-quality audience. Personal email from Steve. Short, direct, includes assessment link. Offer: free upgrade assessment of their current site.

### 3. Social media (organic)
- Post the AI Snapshot concept as content: "Here's what we look at when we assess a small business website for the AI era" → link to assessment
- Instagram/Facebook: "Does your website pass the AI readiness test? Find out free →"
- LinkedIn: Target agency owners and SMBs in the initial focus cities

### 4. Local channels (initial launch cities)
- Hunter Valley / Newcastle / Yarra Valley small business Facebook groups
- Post as a resource, not an ad: "We built a free AI website assessment for Australian small businesses — happy to share if useful"
- Authentic, not promotional

### 5. Advocate / Red Envelope campaign
Advocates give the assessment URL to every prospect conversation:
> "Before we talk about what's possible — let's get you a free snapshot of where your website stands right now."
The assessment becomes the advocate's conversation starter.

### 6. Post-launch paid (Phase 2)
- Facebook/Instagram lead ads targeting AU small business owners, 35-55, in the focus cities
- Google Ads targeting "free website assessment Australia", "AI website check"
- Retarget website visitors who didn't convert

## Secondary Lead Magnets (Phase 2 — post-launch)

These aren't needed for launch but are worth building once the assessment is running:

**1. "AI Readiness Checklist for Australian Small Businesses"**
- Format: PDF checklist (10 items)
- Stage: Awareness (people searching "AI for small business")
- Effort: 2 hours
- Purpose: Top-of-funnel for organic/social traffic; not as high-intent as the assessment

**2. "The Website Update Calculator"**
- Format: Simple calculator (estimated hours/year wasted on developer updates → cost)
- Stage: Consideration (someone comparing options)
- Effort: 4 hours (no-code with Tally or simple HTML)
- Purpose: Makes the cost of NOT switching concrete

**3. "The FWG Quick-Start Guide"**
- Format: Email mini-course (5 emails over 5 days)
- Stage: Decision (someone who got the assessment and wants to learn more before signing up)
- Effort: 2 days
- Purpose: Nurtures warm leads who aren't ready to sign up immediately
