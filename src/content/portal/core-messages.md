# Fast Web Guru — Copy Pillars
*Created: 2026-06-07*
*Sources: website-copy-v2.md, product-marketing.md, customer-research.md, competitor-profiles.md*

---

These 5 pillars are the messaging spine for every piece of Fast Web Guru copy — website, ads, emails, social, and sales conversations. Each one is grounded in confirmed VOC and competitor gaps. Use them to maintain voice and emphasis consistency across any context or author.

---

## Pillar 1: Same-Day Updates

**Core idea:** Website changes happen today — via chat, handled by a real person, with no invoice.

**Why it lands:** The dominant VOC frustration is not the price of changes — it's the wait, the chasing, the re-explaining. "Still waiting two weeks for a simple update" appears across every source. No competitor in the profiled set uses speed as a primary headline. It's unclaimed territory.

**VOC roots:**
- "I just want someone to do it"
- "It should take five minutes"
- "I spend half my day explaining the problem to someone who doesn't know my site"

---

### Headline Variations

**A — Direct/declarative:**
Your website. Done for you. Today.

**B — Problem-first:**
Tired of waiting a week for a five-minute update? We fix it today.

**C — Outcome-focused:**
Update your website from your phone. Done in hours, not weeks.

---

### Body Paragraph

You send a message. We handle it. No ticket queue. No project estimate. No "we'll get back to you with a quote." Just: message in, update live — today. Your dedicated support person already knows your site, your brand, and what you're trying to do. You don't have to explain it every time. That's the bit most services miss.

**Core idea:** AI does the heavy lifting. A real specialist checks every output before it reaches you or your customers.

- "I no longer have to worry about the technical part — I get to focus on what I do best"

AI does the work. A real person makes sure it's right.

## Pillar 5: No Gatekeeping

**Core idea:** Every plan includes the same features and the same AI tools. The only difference is how much human support time is included.

**Why it lands:** Feature-locking is the norm in SaaS and hosting — and Australian SMBs have been burned by it repeatedly. "The $199 is bait" is a real, documented reflex. FWG's no-gatekeeping principle (all tiers same feature access; price = support hours only) is genuinely unusual, clearly defensible, and directly addresses the bait-and-switch anxiety that makes low prices feel dangerous.

**VOC roots:**
- "The $199 is bait... it's just the beginning... be careful"
- "What's not included?"
- "You don't own the site, you often don't own the domain, and if you stop paying, the site disappears"

---

### Headline Variations

**A — Principle frame:**
Every plan. Same features. Same AI. No upsells.

**B — Contrast frame:**
No feature-locking. No premium tier hidden behind a paywall. Just: more support if you want it.

**C — Transparency frame:**
The $40 plan and the $240 plan run the same AI stack. The difference is how many hours we spend with you each month.

Most services charge more to unlock features. We don't. The only thing that changes between our plans is how much of our team's time you get each month: the $40 plan includes one hour a month of dedicated support. The $240 plan includes four hours a week. The AI tools, the hosting infrastructure, the workflow systems, and the features — all identical. If you want to do more yourself, the $40 plan gives you everything you need. If you want us to handle more, the $240 plan gives you a near full-time team. No tricks. No traps. No fine print.

## How to Use These Pillars

**On the website:** Each pillar should appear above the fold or within the first scroll on its most relevant page. Pillars 1 and 5 belong on the homepage. Pillar 2 belongs on the pricing page. Pillars 3 and 4 belong on How It Works and service pages.

**In emails:** Use one pillar per email in the nurture sequence. Don't combine. Email 2 (Day 2) = Same-Day. Email 3 (Day 4) = No Gatekeeping. etc.

**In ads:** Each pillar can anchor a standalone ad. Do not mix pillars within one ad unit.

**In conversation (sales/advocacy):** When someone raises the "is this reliable?" objection → Pillar 4. "Is the cheap price a warning sign?" → Pillar 5. "What's actually AI vs what's human?" → Pillar 3.

**What to avoid:** Combining all 5 in one headline or section. One pillar = one section = one conversion argument.
