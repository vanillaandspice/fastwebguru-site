## Strategic Overview

### Two audiences, two entry paths

**Audience A — The Add-On Seeker**
Has a website already. Frustrated that AI tools don't know their business. Hasn't switched hosting because the thought is too much effort. Budget: happy to pay $30/month without a second thought if the value is immediately clear.

Funnel: Ad → `/cortex` landing page → "Add Cortex to Your Website" sign-up

**Audience B — The Full-Package Buyer**
Wants to start fresh or upgrade from a host they're not happy with. Willing to invest $100+/month and up to $1,500 once-off for a proper website setup. Often frustrated with the developer they currently use, or embarrassed by their current site.

Funnel: Ad → `/cortex/full-package` or free AI assessment → FWG Core + Cortex conversation

---

### Platform roles

| Platform | Role | Audience match |
|----------|------|----------------|
| Google Search | Intent capture — they're searching for AI tools, website help, or hosting | Both A and B |
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | Awareness and education — interrupt the scroll, plant the idea | Primarily B (longer consideration buyers) |
| Google Display / Retargeting | Bring back visitors who landed on FWG or Cortex pages without converting | Both |
| Instagram Stories | Short-form awareness — visual format, $30/month hook | A (impulse-friendly price point) |

---

### Voice rules for Cortex ads

- No exclamation marks (cheapens the brand)
- No buzzwords: "game-changing", "revolutionary", "leverage", "empower"
- Lead with the $30 bundle price or the outcome — not the product name
- "Cortex by Fast Web Guru" — always attribute the brand in longer formats
- Australian voice: plain, direct, occasionally dry. Not salesy.
- Specific beats vague: "20 growth workflows built for your business" beats "powerful marketing tools"

## Ad Performance Notes

### Headlines to test across platforms:
1. "Your website just grew a brain." — curiosity-led; best for warm audiences
2. "AI that actually knows your business." — benefit-led; best for cold search
3. "Three AI upgrades. $30/month bundle." — math-led; best for price-motivated buyers
4. "Stop getting generic AI output." — pain-first; best for retargeting

### Angles to rotate by platform:
- Google Search: pain-first headlines (speak to the active frustration they're searching about)
- Meta Feed: story-driven, social proof anchored (embed real numbers and real situations)
- Retargeting: objection handling (address the specific hesitation signals from page behaviour)
- Stories: single insight + price + CTA (15 seconds, audio off)

### What NOT to say in Cortex ads:
- "Revolutionary AI platform" — hype
- "Leverage AI to grow your business" — corporate filler
- "Unlock the power of artificial intelligence" — meaningless
- "Best AI tool for small business" — unsubstantiated superlative
- "Limited time offer" — not consistent with FWG brand pricing transparency

### Compliance notes:
- All claims (e.g. "20 growth workflows", "more added regularly", "1 hr/month AI Specialist", "$30/month bundle") must be substantiated in the service agreement before being used in ads
- "Australian team" / "Australian AI Specialist" — confirm exact team location definition before going live
- "Cancel anytime" — confirm no hidden notice period in terms before using in ads
