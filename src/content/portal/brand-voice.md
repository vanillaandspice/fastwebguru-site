# Fast Web Guru — Brand Voice Examples
*Created: 2026-06-07*
*Purpose: Let any team member or AI session write on-brand without a full briefing*

---

## How to Use This Document

Read each example set, then write. Don't aim for "too corporate" or "too casual" — aim for the FWG-right version. When in doubt, ask: would a switched-on small business owner who's been burned by a web developer trust this? Would it sound like a knowledgeable mate giving honest advice, or like a company trying to impress?

**FWG voice in three words:** Straight. Warm. Sharp.

**Red flags — cut immediately if you write these:**
- Exclamation marks used for energy
- "Leverage," "optimise," "facilitate," "streamline," "innovative," "cutting-edge"
- Passive voice ("your website will be managed by our team")
- Vague outcomes ("better results," "improved performance," "business growth")
- Sentences over 25 words
- Any paragraph that couldn't be spoken aloud in one breath

## Statement 1: What Fast Web Guru Is

*When to use: hero sections, about page, email intros, partner pitches*

---

**❌ Too Corporate**

Fast Web Guru is a market-leading digital infrastructure platform leveraging AI-enhanced technologies to deliver comprehensive managed website solutions. We empower small and medium enterprises with enterprise-grade capabilities through our innovative, scalable service model — enabling businesses to optimise their digital presence and accelerate growth at an accessible price point.

*What's wrong: "Market-leading," "leverage," "empower," "optimise," "accelerate growth" — every word is borrowed from a pitch deck. Zero specificity. No human in sight. An SMB owner reads this and immediately feels like they're about to get sold something they don't need.*

---

**❌ Too Casual**

Basically we're like your website crew, yeah? So we take care of all the tech stuff — AI, hosting, updates, whatever. It's heaps good value and the team's pretty switched on. You just hit us up when you need something done and we sort it out.

*What's wrong: "Basically," "heaps good," "pretty switched on," "whatever" — sounds like a year 10 student explaining their part-time job. No confidence. No specificity. The slang undermines the technical credibility that makes the price believable.*

---

**✅ FWG-Right**

Fast Web Guru gives Australian small businesses an AI-powered website, same-day updates from a real person, and one fixed monthly price — from $40/month. No invoice every time you want to change something. No chasing. No surprises. Just your website, done for you, by a team that actually knows what they're doing.

*What's right: Specific price. Specific promise. Three "No" lines that resolve the three biggest VOC frustrations. "Actually knows what they're doing" sounds like a friend vouching for someone, not a brand claiming expertise. Short sentences.*

## Statement 2: Same-Day Updates

*When to use: homepage problem/solution section, service pages, email body copy, ads*

---

**❌ Too Corporate**

Our proprietary request management system facilitates rapid response times, with a commitment to delivering website update solutions within the same business day. Our dedicated team of specialists ensures that all client-initiated change requests are processed efficiently and delivered to agreed service level standards.

*What's wrong: "Proprietary request management system," "client-initiated change requests," "service level standards" — nobody talks like this. It buries the most compelling feature (same-day, real person, via chat) under jargon. Also passive: "are processed" instead of "we process."*

---

**❌ Too Casual**

Honestly if you need something changed just chuck us a message and we'll bang it out same day, no worries. Like it's dead easy — you just leave a comment on your site and boom, done. Way better than chasing some dev for two weeks lol.

*What's wrong: "Chuck us a message," "bang it out," "dead easy," "boom," "lol" — the slang level exceeds the brand. "No worries" works in Australian conversation but reads as dismissive in marketing copy. The comparison ("chasing some dev") is good but the delivery undermines it.*

---

**✅ FWG-Right**

Need something changed on your site? Leave a comment — just like a sticky note on the page. We get it, we fix it, it's live today. No quoting process. No back-and-forth. No waiting until next week. Your dedicated support person already knows your site, so you don't have to explain it every time.

*What's right: "Sticky note on the page" is a concrete analogy that makes BugHerd's function immediately understandable. Three parallel "No" lines mirror the VOC pain points. "Already knows your site" directly addresses the "someone who doesn't know my site" complaint from research. Short. Direct.*

## Statement 3: Pricing and Value

*When to use: pricing page, comparison sections, email sequences, ad copy*

---

**❌ Too Corporate**

Our tiered pricing architecture is designed to provide maximum accessibility and scalability, enabling businesses at varying stages of digital maturity to access our full-featured AI-enhanced service offering. Investment commences from just $40 per month, with advanced tiers available to accommodate the evolving needs of growth-oriented organisations.

*What's wrong: "Tiered pricing architecture," "digital maturity," "investment commences," "growth-oriented organisations" — this is MBA-speak. "Investment commences from just $40" manages to make $40 sound expensive. "Just" and "investment" are both classic upsell-language tells that this audience has heard before and distrusts.*

---

**❌ Too Casual**

Look honestly $40/mo is basically nothing right? Like you're probably spending that on a random subscription you forgot about. And the $240 plan is like having a full-time person, except way cheaper. We're not gonna rip you off or hide fees — what you see is what you pay, pinky swear.

*What's wrong: "Basically nothing," "pinky swear" — flippant about price in a way that makes the audience suspicious (echoes of the "bait" pattern they've been burned by). "Random subscription you forgot about" is relatable but makes the service sound trivial. The "we're not gonna rip you off" framing sounds defensive.*

---

**✅ FWG-Right**

$40/month. That's it. No invoice every time you want to change a word. No hidden fees. No upgrade required to access AI tools — they're included at every tier. The $40 plan and the $240 plan run the same AI stack. The only difference is how many hours of our team's time you get each month. We price by support level, not by feature-locking. Because that's how it should work.

*What's right: Leads with the number. "No invoice every time you want to change a word" is direct VOC language. The "same AI stack" explanation is specific and credible — it explains the no-gatekeeping principle without using the phrase. "Because that's how it should work" — short, confident, conversational.*

## Statement 4: AI Capabilities

*When to use: AI tools section, service pages, email body, partner materials*

---

**❌ Too Corporate**

Our cutting-edge AI-powered content generation suite leverages large language model technology to deliver data-driven marketing assets across multiple channels. Seamlessly integrated into your existing workflow ecosystem, our AI capabilities empower your business to scale content production while maintaining brand consistency and reducing time-to-market.

*What's wrong: Almost every word is a red flag — "cutting-edge," "leverage," "seamlessly integrated," "workflow ecosystem," "empower," "scale," "time-to-market." Zero specificity about what AI actually does for the customer. This reads like a vendor pitch from a tech conference.*

---

**❌ Too Casual**

AI is pretty wild these days hey? We've hooked into heaps of the good AI tools — like Claude, GPT, the works — and we just run all that stuff for you so you don't have to figure it out yourself. It spits out content and images and stuff and then our team checks it's all good before it gets to you.

*What's wrong: "Pretty wild," "heaps of," "the works," "spits out," "and stuff" — imprecise language for a technical capability that's being asked to justify a $240/month spend. "Spits out" for an SMB's brand assets is an unfortunate image. The voice is too relaxed for a professional differentiator.*

---

**✅ FWG-Right**

We set up and run the AI tools for you. That means AI-generated content, images, and marketing copy — for your business, in your voice, reviewed by a real human before it reaches you or your customers. You don't need to know what a prompt is. You don't need an account with five different AI platforms. You tell us what you need, we get the AI to produce it, and a specialist on our team checks it's actually right before delivery.

*What's right: "Set up and run for you" — names the service model without explaining the tech. "In your voice" — directly answers the "what if AI gets my brand wrong" objection. "You don't need to know what a prompt is" — the Australian anti-jargon preference. Specific about the human QA step. "Actually right" — casual qualifier that sounds real, not polished.*

## Statement 5: Support and Reliability

*When to use: About page, How It Works, FAQ, email sequences, partnership materials*

---

**❌ Too Corporate**

Our comprehensive managed service offering encompasses a dedicated client success team operating across extended service windows, ensuring maximum uptime and optimal client satisfaction outcomes. With a robust support infrastructure underpinned by triple-redundant failover architecture, we are committed to delivering industry-leading reliability and responsiveness metrics.

*What's wrong: "Client success team," "extended service windows," "optimal client satisfaction outcomes," "underpinned by," "industry-leading reliability and responsiveness metrics" — meaningless corporate promises that could apply to any managed service. The audience has heard every version of this and trusts none of it.*

---

**❌ Too Casual**

So like if anything goes wrong just hit us up and we'll sort it. We've got backup systems and stuff so it doesn't really go down. And you've always got your logins — we'd never be dodgy about that. Our team is pretty chill but they know what they're doing, promise.

*What's wrong: "Pretty chill," "promise," "dodgy," "and stuff" — the reassurance is undermined by the register. "We'd never be dodgy" raises the question of whether they might be. "Hit us up" reads as informal for a service offering 1-hour SLA and 24/7 coverage.*

---

**✅ FWG-Right**

You have a dedicated support person — one person who knows your site, your business, and your preferences. When something needs fixing, they're on it. Not after a ticket queue clears. Not after we figure out which support tier you're on. Now. We're available until 10pm on weekdays and all day on weekends, because that's when small businesses actually need help. And your logins — your site, your domain, your hosting — they're always yours. Full access, no conditions.

*What's right: "One person" — mirrors the VOC ideal relationship ("one person. one number. one fixed price."). "Not after a ticket queue clears" — the contrast does more work than describing what we do. Specific hours ("until 10pm on weekdays") make the SLA concrete. The ownership section ("your site, your domain, your hosting — always yours") resolves Trigger 3 (feeling trapped) with specificity rather than just promising it.*

## Voice Quick-Reference

| If you're tempted to write... | Write this instead |
|-------------------------------|-------------------|
| leverage | use |
| optimise | improve / make better |
| seamlessly | (cut it — if it's seamless, the reader experiences that without being told) |
| innovative | new / different / (just describe what's new) |
| solutions | services / tools / (be specific about what it is) |
| empower | help / give / let |
| drive growth | make the phone ring / get more bookings / bring in leads |
| at scale | for more clients / without adding headcount |
| pain points | problems / frustrations / (use the specific one) |
| industry-leading | (prove it instead — "1-hour SLA, 24/7") |
| investment commences at | starts at / from |
| client | customer / client (fine — but not "client base") |
| utilise | use |
| robust | strong / reliable |
| cutting-edge | new / current / (just describe the tech specifically) |

## The Single Test

Before publishing any piece of FWG copy, ask:

**"Would a switched-on tradie with a $5,000 bad-developer experience trust this?"**

If yes: publish.
If no: cut the jargon, add a specific number or real outcome, and try again.
