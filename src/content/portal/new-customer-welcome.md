# Fast Web Guru — Client Onboarding Flow
## Corey Haines Onboarding Framework

*Created: 2026-06-07 | Source: product-marketing.md + onboarding skill*

---

## The Aha Moment Definition

**FWG's aha moment:** The first time a client comments on their website via BugHerd and sees the change made same-day.

This is the moment that makes the value real. Everything before this is promise. This is proof.

**Secondary aha moments:**
- First time they receive an AI-generated piece of content that's actually good
- First time the support person proactively catches an issue before the client noticed it
- First time they check the site at 9pm on a Sunday and get a response within the hour

**Activation target:** Client completes first BugHerd comment within 48 hours of going live.

## Day-by-Day: The First Week

### Day 0 — Signup day

**FWG team action:** Support person sends introduction email within 4 hours.

**Introduction email template:**

Subject: Hi [First Name] — I'm [Name], your Fast Web Guru support person

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I'm [Name] — I'll be your main point of contact at Fast Web Guru. I'm setting up your account now and will have your website live on the new hosting within the next 24–48 hours.
>
> Before I get into the technical stuff, can I ask a few quick questions? Just reply to this email:
>
> 1. What's the most important thing your website should be doing for your business right now?
> 2. Is there anything on your current site that's been bugging you — something you've been wanting to fix for ages?
> 3. Any upcoming promotions, events, or changes I should know about?
>
> This helps me prioritise the right things from day one.
>
> Talk soon,
> [Name]

---

### Day 1–2 — Technical setup

**FWG team actions:**
- Migrate or set up hosting
- Install BugHerd on the website
- Connect Workflow Hub (email, social, tasks)
- Set up AI tools access
- Send "you're live" confirmation

**"You're live" email:**

Subject: [First Name], you're live on Fast Web Guru

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Your website is now running on Fast Web Guru's infrastructure. Here's what just changed:
>
> ✓ Hosting migrated to our triple-redundant system
> ✓ BugHerd installed — you can start commenting on your site right now
> ✓ Workflow Hub connected — I can see your emails, tasks, and social
>
> **Your next step: make your first BugHerd comment.**
>
> Open your website in a new tab. Click on anything that needs changing — a word, an image, a price, anything. Leave a comment like a sticky note. I'll pick it up within the hour and sort it same-day.
>
> I'll walk you through anything you're unsure about — just reply or comment directly on the site.
>
> [Name]

---

### Day 3 — Workflow Hub walkthrough

**FWG team action:** 20-minute screen share or walkthrough video specific to their setup.

Covers:
1. Where to find everything (tasks, comms, social)
2. How to use BugHerd for website changes
3. How to request AI content
4. How to reach their support person

**FWG action:** Deliver first piece of AI content (social post, product description, or whatever is most relevant to their business type).

---

### Day 7 — Check-in

**Format:** 15-minute call or async video message (Loom)

**Agenda:**
1. How's it going? Any friction points?
2. Here's what we've done this week [recap]
3. Here's what we'd suggest focusing on in week 2
4. Any questions?

**After the call:** Send a written recap with action items.

## Onboarding Email Sequence (separate from assessment nurture)

Triggered on signup. 4 emails over 14 days. Reinforces in-product actions.

**Email 1** (Day 0): Welcome + what happens next *(sent as the welcome email above)*

**Email 2** (Day 2): "How to use BugHerd in 60 seconds"
> Subject: The fastest way to update your website [First Name]
> Short email — one thing: how to leave a BugHerd comment. Link to a 60-second video or GIF demo.

**Email 3** (Day 5): "What your AI tools can do"
> Subject: Here's what we set up in your Workflow Hub
> Walk through 3 specific AI capabilities available to them. One concrete example of each. "You can request any of these from me directly — just reply to this email."

**Email 4** (Day 14): "How's your first fortnight been?"
> Subject: Quick check-in — how's it going, [First Name]?
> Genuine check-in. Ask what's working, what isn't. Invite feedback. Reinforce the relationship.

## Stalled User Recovery

**Definition of stalled:** No BugHerd activity after 5 days of going live.

**Recovery sequence:**
1. Support person sends personal email: "Hey [First Name] — noticed you haven't used BugHerd yet. Is everything working okay? If there's something you've been wanting to change on the site, just mention it here and I'll sort it — even if you never use the tool yourself."
2. If still no activity after 3 more days: quick call offer
3. If no response after 2 weeks: flag for review (retention risk)

**Key insight:** Some clients don't want to learn any new tools — they just want to email their support person. That's fine. The aha moment can happen via email reply, not BugHerd. Flexibility here is important.

## Advanced Plan Onboarding Differences

Advanced clients get the same flow plus:
- Dedicated onboarding call (30 min, not 15)
- Full Workflow Hub setup including comms management
- First AI content batch delivered in week 1 (not just one piece)
- KPI dashboard setup
- Week 2 "strategy review" — what to focus on for next 30 days

## Churn Risk Signals (to monitor in month 1)

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| No BugHerd activity in 5+ days | Personal outreach from support person |
| No email opens in 7+ days | Check email deliverability; try different subject |
| Support person gets no replies | Phone call attempt |
| Client expresses confusion about scope | Immediate clarity call |
| "This isn't what I expected" feedback | Escalate to Steve; review onboarding conversation |
