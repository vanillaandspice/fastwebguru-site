## Part 1: Overview & Vision

### What Is Fast Web Guru

Fast Web Guru (FWG) is an ecommerce portal built on WHMCS, selling hosting, websites, and advanced AI services to small and medium businesses.

**Initial Phase:** The first priority is upgrading FWG's own existing client list from legacy systems to newer AI-powered websites. This phase is primarily a stress test and practical exercise with new software and systems.

**Growth Phase:** After the initial upgrade phase, the primary target market is the development of a strong portal-ecosystem for the wholesale agency market (in order of importance): marketers, copywriters, graphic designers, web developers, systems integrators.

The portal supports multiple vendors — initially the only vendors are Vanilla & Spice (with the Branding Companion) and FWG's own "Business in a Box" hosting/AI range. Over time, third-party software and services from others in the industry who complement FWG will be added.

Suppliers to FWG will get a variation of the $4 or $40 plan to help them use AI themselves for their business. Some will resell these services; others mainly use the portal to support their own services. Most contributors/suppliers to Fast Web Guru should come through VS Creative.

### Background & History

- Fast Web Guru was previously operated by a Filipino business responsible for hosting the Fenwick Hill Websites (now managed directly by Vanilla & Spice)
- FWG operated for about ten years prior to the Fenwick Hill takeover
- All assets and customers were acquired by Vanilla & Spice, and the new Joint Venture was established in mid 2026
- Front-end currently operates on a WordPress website, with the backend powered by WHMCS to manage hosting services
- FWG currently has 20 standard WordPress website hosting clients and 6 internal (non-ecommerce) websites within the Fenwick Hill Group to manage
- FWG also supports Vanilla and Spice with their ecommerce stores management (Triple Scented, HV Market, Hunter Valley Hampers, and Rico Coco Chocolate Tea — note: Rico Coco and Hunter Valley Hampers products are sold exclusively via HV Market; all stores are WooCommerce and WordPress)
- Currently FWG only has one ecommerce client — Marcos Pizza — but Vanilla and Spice also has some which may be combined into a single group and offered via a Fast Web Guru package, with Vanilla and Spice components provided as part of that package (TBC and planned)

### The $4 MVP Philosophy

A core concept of FWG is the MVP $4 concept — all tiers of service start with the minimum high-end AI service which can be provided, capped at a maximum COST of $4 per month. Zero profit margin on this level. Utilising strict cost controls and automation, proving what can be achieved for only $1 per week, and then building upwards from there, encourages an approach of sustainability — something useful for charities and those in developing countries, but it also gives FWG a very low cost base on which to base the $40 package which is identical just has extra labour included. This utilises local models, free and open-source software, and processes and systems developed by FWG and VS at cost (i.e. what is the compute cost of offering them if there was zero royalty/return — likewise if any labout is needed to work on this, then what does it look like for 30 minutes of labour at cost).

The MVP concept is NOT promoted at all. The $4 tier is a hidden, invite-only section for charities, team members, selected vendors, and those with a voucher code. For some people it is an intro only and after 12 weeks jumps to the $40 plan; for others it is ongoing (e.g. charities). The landing pages for this area will emphasise the exclusivity of being selected and getting 90% of the price of the $40 package covered by Fast Web Guru — effectively the $4 package and the $40 package need to be the same. By engineering our $40 package to have costs under 10%, we can achieve a "special entry" tier at $4.

This concept also forces the team to design viable options for smaller businesses who cannot afford or do not want to add-on a bunch of API keys and extra AI costs — what does their MVP look like with our system? Can they run it with only free Claude and free ChatGPT? This is where Fast Web Guru's investment in infrastructure comes in — we already have servers and AI at a high level IF needed to complement the 99% of activities which can be carried out with low-cost software. Our high tech stack allows us to distribute the benefits of this over hundreds of hosting and AI clients who all benefit from the sharing of these high-level software applications, and having access to the same team and a similar AI stack to what people spending $1,000+ a month have.

### The "No Gatekeeping" Principle

A core concept behind FWG is that the different tiers are NOT gatekeeping and feature-locking. ALL tiers share the same features. Pricing tiers are based on how much external support clients want (the labour aspects) and a simple "With AI or Without AI" option at each tier — but this with-or-without-AI option should also be identical across all tiers. The only core difference is how much hand-holding and support is needed vs how much DIY. The $4 package is the ultimate DIY (for those invited — as there is no budget for labour at this level apart from a tiny amount), and the $240 per month gives people a solid labour force to supervise and implement all aspects.

Every client benefits from the AI even if it is just an initial research and document library provided to them when they start (for the non-AI clients).

The AI clients would get full AI briefings, .md files, design files, and core workflows they can use in their own AI to regenerate these reports at will — duplicates of coreyhaines and internal systems PLUS actual integrations of their AI tool with their website and assets if that is what they want.

They may also require initial setup GitHub MVP linkages etc. So we should have a template automation to setup from scratch entire tech-stack. This is our most important asset.

## Part 4: Core Market Problems Addressed

The organisation directly solves several systemic issues in the market:

- **Online Infrastructure & Tech Management:** Entrepreneurs lack CTOs/COOs to manage software selection, email, and e-commerce costs. They often rely on overpriced, under-skilled web developers who incorrectly configure WordPress plugins and perform basic e-commerce updates slowly, charging full development rates.
- **Content Creation Bottlenecks:** Creating daily, unique push-marketing content is too time-consuming. External agencies are expensive and generic, while internal teams lack scalable skills.
- **Labour Inefficiencies:** Traditional Virtual Assistants (VAs) lack deep e-commerce/tech skills. VAs, agencies, and developers have a direct conflict of interest regarding efficiency: they have no incentive to use new technology to speed up work or lower costs, as maximising billable hours maximises their profit.
- **Retail & Manufacturing Deficiencies:** Food businesses struggle with stock-outs and waste due to poor volume and failing to use scanners. They lack backup resilience, website security, and after-hours support. Crucially, they lack promotional materials (posters, banners, shelf-talkers, takeaway recipe cards) and digital assets (12 social posts per product, recipes). Finally, successful wholesalers often abandon tradeshows and in-store tastings, losing vital brand awareness and physical retail support.

## Part 6: Sales & Marketing Strategy

VS rejects traditional SaaS or ad agency models, acting instead as a high-end, bespoke marketing collateral firm.

### The Advocate Model

Self-employed freelancers/specialists act as localised in-house reps. Advocates operate with transparent profit shares and no hidden VS corporate agendas. An advocate can visit a business, spend 2 hours photographing and interviewing staff, and immediately generate custom sample materials via mobile phone and a portable A4 printer to pitch the client on the spot.

Advocates are contracted direct via Fast Web Guru (not via VS or VS Creative) — they are independent of the other profit centres and owe their primary loyalty to the client. Fast Web Guru is just the processing engine and their support team to run their own advocacy business, as any other third-party supplier to FWG would when using our ecommerce and support systems.

### Wholesale Partnerships

Targeting agencies and copywriters who can onboard their entire client base. Partners bringing in 4+ clients/month earn $2,000–$4,000 in commissions per 4 clients. If a partner drops below 12 sales per calendar year, they are relegated to a half-rate referral fee.

### The "Red Envelope" Viral Campaign

Highly personalised, localised, and sensuous wax-sealed red envelopes are delivered (by advocates or courier) to prospective businesses. These contain custom marketing samples, handwritten notes, and local gifts (e.g. art or food with a deep local story, tying into charitable initiatives like **Daauw Village**, a Laos charity Steve supports that helps poor village women and children). Crucially, the envelopes contain *no selling, pricing, or covert manipulation* — only a phone number or QR code for verification. This aims to foster intense curiosity, FOMO, and a lottery-like attraction, prompting organic viral sharing and reviews from grateful business owners.

### Crowdfunding (Exploratory)

An exploratory idea to potentially crowdfund the launch of the app rental to users in the future.

## Part 7: Deliverables & Quality Assurance

The service provides unparalleled marketing collateral and brand asset libraries.

- **Asset Packages:** Each product/article receives a series of 10 unique images, provided in multiple formats (web, email, stories, reels, posts, banners). This yields 60 total images per series (30 with CTAs/overlays, 30 blank), delivered in high-quality PNG and small JPG formats.
- **Content Breakdown:** A core series includes 2 USP/buy-now graphics, 2 testimonial/story graphics, 2 target market graphics, and 4 sales/offer graphics. These are evergreen assets that update landing pages and enhance sales funnels indefinitely.
- **Quality Control (QA):** Output is reviewed in real-time by a panel of 4–5 designers, copywriters, and marketers. Reviewers are paid $20 per 20 minutes to critique every series, ensuring supreme brand alignment. This rigorous custom QA combined with AI means the ads are impossible to recreate using competitor software like **Nano Banana** (an image creation software built by Google).
