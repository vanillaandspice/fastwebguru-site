## Value Metric Analysis

**FWG's value metric: human support time (hours/month)**

This is the right value metric for FWG because:
- It scales with the value customers receive (more support = more done)
- It's easy to understand ("you get 1 hour of our team's time")
- It aligns with the "no gatekeeping" positioning (features are the same — you're buying labour)
- It's honest and transparent (a core brand value)

**Implication:** The pricing page should lead with this framing. Not "Core vs Advanced" but "how much of our team do you want?"

## Pricing Psychology Assessment

### Anchoring — Current state: Weak
The jump from $40 → $240 is 6x. That's a large gap with nothing in between, which creates a binary choice (too cheap / too expensive) rather than a natural upgrade path.

**Recommendation:** The $240 plan should feel like the obvious upgrade for growing businesses. The current gap is too large — it creates sticker shock. Consider:
- Option A: Keep as-is but frame $240 more aggressively as "your dedicated weekly team"
- Option B: Add a $120/mo mid-tier (8 hrs/mo) between Core and Advanced *(requires Brand DNA approval — not in current plan)*
- **Recommended for launch:** Keep as-is (don't introduce new tiers without business approval) but anchor the $240 price harder against what 16 hrs of freelance web work would cost ($1,200–$2,400/mo at typical AU rates)

### The Decoy Effect — Not currently used
The pricing page has two "real" tiers ($40 and $240). For decoy pricing to work there needs to be a middle tier that makes the target tier look like the obvious choice. Since we're not adding a new tier, we can manufacture this psychologically by:
- Making the AI Extender (+$450) visible as an option — which makes $240 look more reasonable by comparison
- Showing the "value equivalent" (what 16 hrs of agency/freelancer time would cost elsewhere)

### Charm vs Round Pricing
$40 and $240 are round numbers — right for FWG's premium, transparent positioning. Don't switch to $39/$239. Round numbers signal confidence and honesty, which matches the brand voice.

### Annual Discount — Not currently defined
**Recommendation:** Offer 2 months free on annual (= ~17% discount, industry standard).
- Core: $400/year (vs $480 monthly) — saves $80
- Advanced: $2,400/year (vs $2,880 monthly) — saves $480
- Frame as: "Pay annually, get 2 months free" — specific is better than "17% off"
- Annual plans should unlock full year's hours immediately (already in the Brand DNA)

## Pricing Page Architecture Recommendations

### What to show publicly:
1. **Core** ($40/mo) — highlighted as the starting point
2. **Advanced** ($240/mo) — highlighted as "most popular" or "best for ecommerce"
3. **Website Builds** — separate section, once-off prices
4. **AI Extender** — shown as add-on, not a standalone tier
5. *$4 Invite tier* — NOT shown

### Recommended plan card hierarchy:
```
[Core — $40/mo]          [Advanced — $240/mo]  ← "Most Popular" badge
1 hr/mo support           16 hrs/mo support
Ideal for: service         Ideal for: ecommerce +
businesses, simple sites   busy operators
```

### The "no gatekeeping" statement must appear above the tier cards:
> "Both plans include the same AI tools, the same tech stack, and the same support team. The only difference is how many hours of our time you get each month."

This is a powerful differentiator. WP Engine, Kinsta — nobody says this. It should be visually prominent, not buried.

### Anchoring line for Advanced:
> "16 hours of dedicated support per month. That's what a web developer charges for 2 basic jobs — except your Fast Web Guru team handles everything, 24/7, all month."

Au freelance dev rates in this space: $75–$150/hr. 16 hours = $1,200–$2,400/mo. Framing $240 against this makes it land as extraordinary value.

## Website Build Pricing Assessment

Current structure:
- $240 / $1,200 / $2,400 (standard)
- $4,800 / $7,600 / $9,000 (ecommerce)

### Naming the tiers:
Right now they're just price points. Giving them names helps visitors self-select:
- $240 — "Starter" (up to 5 pages, simple service sites)
- $1,200 — "Standard" (most business websites)
- $2,400 — "Custom" (complex requirements, scoped separately)
- $4,800 — "Ecommerce Starter" (new or migrating stores)
- $7,600 — "Ecommerce Standard"
- $9,000 — "Ecommerce Custom" (by quote)

### The "20% human specialist" rule as a differentiator:
This is genuinely unusual in the market and should be on the builds page. Frame it as:
> "We have a hard rule: at least 20% of every build budget goes to a human specialist reviewing and refining the work. On a $1,200 build, that's $240 of dedicated specialist time — reviewing every page, checking brand alignment, ensuring it's not just functional but genuinely good."
This directly counters the objection that "AI just churns out generic websites."

## Pricing FAQ Additions

Based on pricing framework, add to the FAQ section of the pricing page:

**Q: Why is $40/mo so cheap compared to agencies?**
> "Because we engineer our costs like a product, not a service. Our system is built to run at maximum efficiency — automation handles the volume, specialists handle the judgement calls. We pass that efficiency on to you instead of padding margins."

**Q: What if I need more than my monthly hours?**
> "You can purchase additional hours at our standard rate, or upgrade to the next plan. Annual clients can also borrow against next month's allocation."

**Q: What happens to unused hours?**
> "Monthly hours are use-it-or-lose-it each month. Annual hours are pooled for the year — use them in any combination."

**Q: Is there a trial?**
> "There's no free trial — but there's also no lock-in. Month-to-month plans can be cancelled any time. And before you commit to anything, you can get a free AI assessment of your current website with no obligation."
