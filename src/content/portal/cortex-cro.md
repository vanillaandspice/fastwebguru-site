# Cortex — CRO Report
*Fast Web Guru | Created: 2026-06-09*
*Scope: Conversion strategy and recommendations for the Cortex landing page, homepage integration, and pricing page update*

---

## Conversion Goals

**Primary goal:**
Cortex sign-up at $30/month (add to existing website or as part of FWG plan)

**Secondary goal:**
FWG Core + Cortex inquiry ($70/month or new website build + Cortex)

**Lead capture fallback:**
"Get Your Free AI Assessment" — feeds the same email nurture sequence; Cortex is positioned as the natural next step after the assessment

**Success metrics to track:**
- `/cortex` page → sign-up conversion rate
- Homepage Cortex section → `/cortex` page click-through rate
- Pricing page add-on block → Cortex sign-up conversion
- Assessment submissions that mention Cortex in follow-up emails

---

## The Two Conversion Funnels

### Funnel A — "$30 Add-On" (primary, highest volume)
*For new visitors who want AI without switching anything*

1. Entry: Ad / search / referral → `/cortex` landing page
2. Page read: Problem → Three pillars → Works on any host
3. Decision: "Add Cortex to Your Website" CTA
4. Sign-up: Account creation / add to existing hosting
5. Onboarding: Business DNA Workshop scheduled

**Key conversion barrier:** "Is this going to work with my specific setup?"
**CRO lever:** The "Works with your existing host" section + FAQ must be visible before the main CTA

---

### Funnel B — "Full Package" (secondary, higher value)
*For new visitors who want the whole thing — website + hosting + AI*

1. Entry: Ad (full-package angle) / search / referral → `/cortex` or `/cortex/full-package`
2. Page read: Full package value equation ($70/mo or $1,200 build + $70/mo)
3. Decision: "Get Your Free AI Assessment" CTA (lower friction entry for higher-value buyers)
4. Assessment: Personalised findings delivered within 24 hrs
5. Follow-up: FWG team initiates conversation → Core + Cortex sign-up

**Key conversion barrier:** "I need a new website too — where does this leave me?"
**CRO lever:** Explicitly address the "new website + Cortex" path on the full-package variant

## Page-by-Page CRO Analysis

### 1. `/cortex` — Standalone Landing Page

---

#### Hero Section

**Assessment:** The hero has two jobs: immediately communicate what Cortex is and deliver the price before the visitor can leave. The H1 "Your website just grew a brain" is punchy and memorable — it's the hook from the briefing's own copy. Good.

**Risk:** "Your website just grew a brain" is a metaphor, not an explanation. Visitors who arrive cold from search may need a fraction of a second more to orient. The subheadline does this work — it must be visible above the fold on mobile.

**Recommendations:**

1. **On mobile:** Keep the subheadline at 2 lines max. Truncation kills orientation. The phrase "Three AI upgrades for your business, at $10 each" must be visible without scrolling.

2. **Price badge placement:** The `$30/month` chip in the pre-headline badge should appear before the headline — not below it. Visitors primed by a "$30/month" anchor will read the headline differently (relief, not skepticism).

3. **A/B test the H1:**
   - Variant A (current): "Your website just grew a brain."
   - Variant B: "AI that actually knows your business. $30 a month."
   - Variant B may outperform for cold traffic; A may outperform for retargeting/warm traffic

4. **Primary CTA label:** "Add Cortex to Your Website — $30/Month" is direct and specific. Good. Test against: "Start for $30/Month" (shorter) and "Get Cortex — $30/Month" (brand-led). Shorter CTAs often convert better on mobile.

5. **Secondary CTA:** The assessment fallback must be visually below the primary CTA — not competing. Use a text link, not a button, so hierarchy is clear.

---

#### Problem Section

**Assessment:** "Your website is doing one job. It should be doing three." is strong — it introduces the three-pillar logic naturally and positions Cortex as a solution to a structural problem, not a feature upgrade.

**Recommendations:**

1. **This section needs social proof immediately after it.** The problem section creates emotional resonance — that's when a quote from a real customer who felt this way lands hardest. Place a pull quote here once testimonials are available.

   Interim placeholder:
   > "I was paying for hosting and getting nothing from it. My website just sat there."
   > — *[Placeholder — source from first Cortex clients]*

2. **Consider adding a single VOC phrase as a pull quote within the problem section itself:**
   > "Paid $3,000 for a website. Not one lead."
   This phrase appears verbatim in customer research (Tradie Web Guys). It crystallises the problem faster than body copy.

3. **The problem section should not exceed 4 sentences.** Current draft is within this range — maintain on all edits.

---

#### Three Pillars Section

**Assessment:** The three pillars are the core of the page. They need to convert each pain point to a deliverable. Current copy achieves this.

**Layout recommendation:** Display the three pillars as a **3-card vertical stack on mobile, 3-column grid on desktop**. Each card needs:
- Pillar name + price ($10) in the card header
- H3 headline (the customer-language hook)
- 2–3 sentence explanation in plain English
- A bulleted "what you get" list (3–4 items)

**Critical recommendation — lead each card with the pain, not the product:**

| Card | Current opening | Recommended opening |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| Host Systems | "Standard web hosting does one thing..." | Lead with: "Still editing your website the old way?" or start with the Talk-to-Edit outcome |
| Team Stack | "Generic AI tools don't know your brand." | This is already pain-first. Keep it. |
| Ops Grid | "Here's the problem with most AI tools..." | This is also pain-first. Keep it. |

Host Systems is the weakest card for pain-first opening. The current line explains what standard hosting does — the customer doesn't need that explanation, they need to feel the "what if you didn't have to do it that way?" moment first.

**Revised Host Systems opening:**
> "You know what needs updating on your site. You just have nowhere to put it without a developer.
> Cortex Host Systems fixes this. Point at any part of your site and type what you want. The AI handles it — no code, no builders, no quoting."

4. **Price displayed per card:** "$10/month" must appear in the card header or first line. Do not put it only at the bottom. Visitors scan vertically — they need the price anchored to each value delivery point, not summarised once at the end.

---

#### The $30 Equation Block

**Assessment:** This is the highest-converting block on the page if presented correctly. The math is elegant and the comparison to streaming subscriptions is exactly the right frame (familiar cost, zero business ROI).

**Recommendations:**

1. **Make this a visually distinct callout** — contrasting background, slightly larger type on the math, prominent placement. It's the single most shareable, memorable thing on the page.

2. **Add a direct comparison:**
   > Netflix ($23/mo) — 0 leads, 0 workflows, 0 AI for your business.
   > Cortex ($30/mo) — AI infrastructure, brand-trained brain, 20 growth workflows.
   This framing ("you're already spending this") removes the "is it worth it?" resistance before it forms.

3. **Don't use this block as the primary CTA placement** — it's a value confirmation block, not a decision block. Place it between the pillar section and the "Works with your existing host" section.

---

#### "Works with your existing host" Section

**Assessment:** This is a critical trust and accessibility section — it removes the #1 barrier for new visitors ("I'd have to switch everything"). It must be present and prominent.

**CRO problem:** The current section ends with a callout toward FWG hosting ("Better on FWG"). This is the right instinct but the framing needs to be soft — not a hard upsell.

**Recommendations:**

1. **Lead with the zero-barrier message:** "You don't have to switch anything to start."
2. **Be specific about what works on any host vs what's FWG-only:**

   | Feature | Any host | FWG hosting |
   |---------|---------|-------------|
   | Business DNA Workshop | ✓ Full | ✓ Full |
   | Digital Marketing Brain | ✓ Full | ✓ Full |
   | AI Specialist (1 hr/mo) | ✓ Full | ✓ Full |
   | 20 Growth Workflows | ✓ Full | ✓ Full |
   | Talk-to-Edit (site editing by prompt) | Partial | ✓ Full |

   This table converts skeptics who think "it won't work properly on my host." Most of Cortex works everywhere — only Host Systems' Talk-to-Edit runs at full power on FWG infrastructure.

3. **The FWG hosting callout should use natural curiosity, not a sales push:**
   > "Most Cortex clients eventually move their site to FWG hosting — not because they have to, but because having AI infrastructure integrated from the ground up is noticeably faster. When you're ready to look at that, [see FWG hosting plans →]. No rush."

---

#### Pricing Block

**Assessment:** The pricing table (three pillars, $10 each) is strong. The clarity of $10/pillar is the best pricing communication this product has.

**Recommendations:**

1. **Make the table interactive on desktop** — hovering a pillar row reveals a one-line description of what it does. Reduces visual weight while keeping full information available.

2. **Annual pricing:** Offer annual at 2 months free ($300/year instead of $360). Show both month/year options as a toggle above the table — consistent with the rest of the FWG pricing page.

3. **Trust signals directly below the CTA:**
   - "Month-to-month. Cancel anytime."
   - "No setup fee."
   - "Works on your existing host from day one."
   - "Australian AI Specialist included — not a bot."
   These go immediately below the "Add Cortex" button, before the FAQ.

4. **"Best on FWG hosting" note:** Appears once, below the trust signals, as a soft recommendation — not a gating message.

---

#### FAQ Section

**Assessment:** FAQ placement at the bottom is standard. For Cortex, the FAQ does important conversion work because the product is new and has unfamiliar concepts (Business DNA, workflows). It needs to be easy to scan.

**Recommendations:**

1. **Lead with the highest-barrier FAQ:** "Does Cortex work on my existing WordPress site?" — this is the #1 friction point for new visitors. It should be Question 1.

2. **Use a simple open/close accordion** — not a wall of text. Each question should be scannable as a headline; visitor opens only what they need.

3. **Consider a "Cortex isn't for you if..." FAQ** — counterintuitive but effective. Acknowledging who Cortex doesn't suit builds credibility with the people it does suit.

4. **Add a "What happens after I sign up?" FAQ** — the onboarding process (Business DNA Workshop) is unfamiliar. Telling visitors exactly what the first week looks like removes a significant "what am I getting into?" anxiety.

   Suggested answer:
   > "Day 1: You get a welcome from your AI Specialist and a short intake form about your business. Day 2–3: We run the Business DNA Workshop — usually a 45-minute session where we gather your documents, style guides, and brand notes. Day 5–7: Your Brand DNA file is ready, your Digital Marketing Brain is live, and your first workflow batch is in progress. Week 2: Your first 20 workflows are delivered."

### 2. Homepage Cortex Section

**Assessment:** The homepage Cortex section is a discovery and handoff block — its job is to make visitors curious about Cortex and click through to `/cortex`, not to fully explain the product.

**CRO recommendations:**

1. **Keep it to 3 bullet points max** — this is not the sales page; it's the door. The three pillar bullets are right. Don't expand them.

2. **The "$10/pillar" math must appear here in a single line** — the best hook for homepage discovery.

3. **CTA label for this section:** "See Cortex — $30/Month →" (link, not button). Button on the homepage is reserved for the global "Get Your Free AI Assessment" CTA. This internal navigation link should be visually distinct but not competing with the primary CTA.

4. **Position in page flow:** After the plans section, before the partner callout. This sequence says: "Here are the plans → here's the AI upgrade layer → here's the B2B opportunity." Logical flow.

5. **Mobile:** The Cortex section should collapse to a clean 2-line summary + CTA on mobile. Do not repeat all three pillar bullets on small screens — use only the hook line and price, then link.

---

### 3. Pricing Page Cortex Add-On Block

**Assessment:** The pricing page add-on block is where "interested but not yet convinced" visitors land. They've seen the plans and they're evaluating whether to add Cortex. The copy must be crisp and the math must be front and centre.

**CRO recommendations:**

1. **Lead the add-on block with the math, not the name:**
   > "$10 + $10 + $10 = $30/month — AI infrastructure, brand-trained brain, 20 monthly workflows."
   This one line answers "what do I get for $30" immediately. The pillar detail follows.

2. **Visual hierarchy:** Each pillar in the add-on block should have a price badge ($10/mo) that's visually prominent. Don't bury it in the body text.

3. **"Included with Advanced" must be a positive statement, not a footnote:**
   Current: *Already on Advanced? Cortex is included at no extra charge.*
   This is good. Keep it — and add it as a badge or highlighted line on the Advanced plan card itself (e.g. "Cortex included" badge).

4. **The add-on block CTA should use the same label as the main Cortex page:** "Add Cortex to Your Plan — $30/Month" — consistency reduces friction for visitors who've been to both pages.

5. **"Works on other hosts too" must appear in the pricing block** — the pricing page gets visitors who aren't yet on FWG hosting. If they see Cortex and think "I'd have to switch hosting to get this," they'll leave. A single line resolves this:
   > "Already on another host? Cortex works there too. [Learn more →](/cortex)"

## Mobile-Specific Notes

- Hero: H1 should be ≤ 6 words (current: 6 — good)
- Pillar cards: Stack vertically. Horizontal 3-col grid collapses at <768px
- $30 equation block: Single line. Math should not wrap onto two lines.
- FAQ: Accordion only. Never open-text FAQ on mobile.
- Primary CTA: Sticky at bottom of screen (appears after user scrolls past hero)
  - **Label for sticky CTA:** "Add Cortex — $30/mo" (shorter for sticky bar)
  - Show sticky CTA from 300px scroll depth
  - Hide when primary CTA is visible in viewport

## Post-Sign-Up Optimisation (Onboarding CRO)

The conversion doesn't end at sign-up — the Business DNA Workshop is a key activation moment. If a client signs up for Cortex but doesn't complete their DNA Workshop, the product has no foundation and churn risk is high.

**Onboarding funnel recommendations:**

1. **Immediate confirmation email:** Confirm sign-up, set expectation for Day 1 contact from AI Specialist. Subject: "Cortex is on. Your AI Specialist will be in touch within 24 hours."

2. **Day 1 touchpoint:** AI Specialist emails/calls to schedule the Business DNA Workshop. Frame it as: "This is the part where we make Cortex actually work for your business — shouldn't take more than 45 minutes."

3. **Workshop completion = activation metric.** Track: % of Cortex sign-ups who complete the DNA Workshop within 7 days. Target: >80%. Below 60% = onboarding copy problem.

4. **First workflow delivery = retention signal.** Clients who receive and review their first workflow batch are significantly more likely to stay. Track time-to-first-workflow.

5. **30-day check-in:** AI Specialist reviews: which workflows were executed, what results were seen, what needs refining in the Brand DNA. This is the moment to upsell toward FWG hosting if they're on an external host.
